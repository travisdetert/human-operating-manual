# The Neurodivergent Contract

### When Your Brain Doesn't Run the Default Operating System

---

## Why Standard Advice Fails

Every productivity book, every self-help system, every "just make a habit" framework is designed for neurotypical brains. They assume:

- You can sustain attention on a chosen task for extended periods
- Your sense of time is roughly accurate
- Starting tasks requires minimal activation energy
- Habits form through simple repetition
- Emotional responses are proportional to stimuli
- You can prioritize effectively between competing demands

If you have ADHD, autism, or other neurodivergent conditions, some or all of these assumptions are wrong. Not because you're broken -- because your brain has a different architecture. Running neurotypical software on neurodivergent hardware doesn't work. It crashes, and then you blame yourself for the crash.

**"My Contract" can work for neurodivergent brains, but it needs to be calibrated.** This module provides that calibration.

---

## ADHD: The Executive Function Gap

ADHD is not an attention deficit. It is a **regulation deficit.** People with ADHD can often hyperfocus for hours on interesting tasks while being unable to start a boring-but-important task that takes 5 minutes. The issue isn't attention -- it's the ability to direct attention where it needs to go.

### What's Actually Happening in the ADHD Brain

**Dopamine:** ADHD brains have lower baseline dopamine levels and less efficient dopamine signaling. Dopamine is the neurotransmitter of motivation, reward anticipation, and "this matters, pay attention." When dopamine is low, everything feels equally unimportant -- or equally overwhelming.

**Executive function:** The prefrontal cortex (your brain's project manager) is underactive in ADHD. Executive functions affected:
- **Working memory:** Holding information while using it ("I went to the kitchen for... what?")
- **Task initiation:** Starting a task feels like pushing through wet concrete
- **Task switching:** Getting pulled into something new is effortless; returning to the original task is nearly impossible
- **Time perception:** Time blindness -- 5 minutes and 2 hours feel the same
- **Emotional regulation:** Emotions hit harder and resolve slower
- **Planning/Prioritization:** Everything feels urgent or nothing does

### How Standard Contract Terms Break for ADHD

| Standard Term | Why It Breaks | ADHD-Calibrated Version |
|---|---|---|
| "Track spending daily" | Requires daily initiation of a boring task. Will be forgotten or avoided within a week | "I will auto-categorize spending through my banking app and review it once per week for 10 minutes on Sunday" |
| "Exercise 4x/week" | Requires planning, preparation, and initiation 4 separate times | "I will put on workout shoes immediately after my morning coffee. The only commitment is putting on shoes. If I exercise after that, great. If not, the shoes were the win" |
| "Meal prep on Sundays" | Multi-step, time-consuming, boring task with delayed reward. ADHD nightmare | "I will buy 5 pre-made salads/meals and 5 frozen backup meals during my weekly grocery trip. Prep when inspired, pre-made when not" |
| "Weekly review every Sunday at 10 AM" | Time-specific recurring commitment with no external accountability or novelty | "Weekly review happens every Sunday. I set 3 phone alarms (9:45, 9:55, 10:00). The review document opens automatically. Time limit: 10 minutes maximum -- not minimum" |
| "No impulse purchases over $50" | ADHD impulse control is neurologically impaired | "All online shopping carts have a 24-hour wait. I remove saved credit cards from all sites. Physical friction replaces willpower I don't have" |

### ADHD-Specific Contract Strategies

**1. Externalize Everything**

ADHD working memory is unreliable. Stop trying to remember things and start writing them down. Not in a system you have to maintain -- on sticky notes, phone notifications, a whiteboard by the door. The system must be simpler than your brain, not more complex.

- Contract terms: written on a card in your wallet or taped to your bathroom mirror
- Bills: all on autopay. Every. Single. One.
- Appointments: in phone calendar with 3 reminders (day before, 1 hour before, 15 minutes before)
- Tasks: visible. Not in an app you'll forget to open. On a whiteboard, on the fridge, on a sticky note on your laptop

**2. Use Body Doubling**

"Body doubling" is working alongside another person (even virtually) to externalize accountability. The other person doesn't do anything -- they just exist in the same space. This works remarkably well for ADHD task initiation.

- Study/work with a friend (in person or on video call)
- Use body doubling apps or Focusmate (virtual co-working)
- Go to a coffee shop or library to work (the social environment provides ambient accountability)

**3. Reduce Activation Energy to Near Zero**

The hardest part of any task with ADHD is starting. The contract term should target the start, not the finish:

- "I will open my budgeting app" (not "I will balance my budget")
- "I will put on my running shoes" (not "I will run 3 miles")
- "I will sit in the chair at my desk" (not "I will work for 2 hours")

Once started, ADHD brains often continue. The barrier is initiation, not execution.

**4. Embrace the Hyperfocus**

Hyperfocus is ADHD's superpower when directed intentionally. If you find yourself in a hyperfocus state on a contract-aligned activity (deep-cleaning the kitchen, working on a skill, exercising), ride it. Don't interrupt it because "it's been an hour."

But beware hyperfocus on non-contract activities (gaming, scrolling, a Wikipedia rabbit hole). Set timers as circuit breakers. Not to stop you -- to make you consciously choose whether to continue.

**5. Shorter Sprints**

4-week sprints may be too long for ADHD. Novelty wears off faster, and the finish line feels too distant. Options:
- 2-week sprints (with a 5-minute review instead of 15)
- Weekly commitments that roll into a monthly review
- Daily commitments only ("Today I will ____") reviewed each morning

## Autism: Structure Is Your Friend (But the Right Structure)

Many autistic people thrive with structure -- more so than neurotypical people. The contract methodology can work exceptionally well for autistic brains because it IS a system. It has rules, patterns, and predictability.

But it needs specific adjustments:

### Sensory Load as System Load

Autistic individuals often experience sensory overload that neurotypical people don't register. Bright lights, noise, textures, crowds, and temperature can consume processing capacity that the contract assumes is available for decision-making and habit execution.

**Contract consideration:** Add sensory management as a contract term if relevant:
- "I will wear noise-canceling headphones in overwhelming environments"
- "I will build in 30 minutes of quiet decompression time after socially demanding activities"
- "I will not schedule more than [X] social commitments per week"

### Routine Rigidity: Asset and Liability

Strong routine adherence is an autistic strength for contract compliance. Once a habit is established, it tends to stick hard. But routine rigidity also means disruptions (travel, schedule changes, unexpected events) can be catastrophic.

**Contract adjustment:** Build disruption protocols into the contract:
- "If my routine is disrupted by [travel/illness/schedule change], my fallback terms are: [2 simplified terms]"
- Explicitly plan for transitions and changes of routine rather than hoping to "adapt"

### Special Interests as Skill Capital

Autistic special interests -- deep, sustained fascination with specific topics -- are a career superpower when channeled. A special interest in programming, data, writing, mechanics, or any marketable domain can produce expert-level proficiency faster than neurotypical interest levels.

**Contract term:** "I will dedicate [X] hours per week to [special interest] specifically as skill capital development." This isn't indulgence -- it's strategic investment in your highest-performing cognitive domain.

### Social Energy Budgeting

Social interaction costs autistic individuals more energy than neurotypical people. This is not social anxiety (though that can coexist) -- it is the cognitive cost of processing social information that neurotypical brains handle automatically.

**Contract consideration:** Budget social energy like money:
- How many social hours can you sustain per week before depletion?
- Which social interactions are mandatory (work, family) and which are discretionary?
- What recovery time do you need after social engagement?
- Build the recovery time INTO the schedule, not as an afterthought

## Medication Within the Contract

If you take medication for ADHD, depression, anxiety, or other conditions, it is a contract term. Not optional. Not "when I remember."

**Contract term:** "I will take my medication every day at [time], [method of reminder: phone alarm, pill organizer, taped to coffee maker]."

### For ADHD Medication Specifically

Stimulant medication (Adderall, Vyvanse, Ritalin, Concerta) provides a window of improved executive function. This window is your highest-leverage time.

**Contract strategy:** Schedule your most important contract activities -- financial review, skill development, meal prep planning, difficult conversations -- during your medication's active period. Don't waste the window on easy tasks or scrolling.

Non-stimulant options (Strattera, Wellbutrin, guanfacine) work differently -- they provide consistent baseline support rather than a peak window. Adjust your schedule accordingly.

**Medication is not cheating.** A person with poor vision wears glasses. A person with diabetes takes insulin. A person with ADHD may need medication to access executive functions that neurotypical people take for granted. The contract works WITH medication, not instead of it.

## Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD)

Common in ADHD, RSD is an intense emotional response to perceived rejection or criticism. It can make the breach protocol feel devastating instead of diagnostic. "I failed my contract term" triggers a shame spiral that can last hours or days.

**Modified breach protocol for RSD:**

1. **Name the breach factually** (same as standard)
2. **Immediately read this statement** (write it on your contract): "A breach is data, not a verdict. I am not a failure. The system had a gap. I am finding the gap."
3. **Skip the emotional processing in the moment.** Log the breach and do the analysis later (the next day, or during weekly review) when the RSD spike has passed
4. **One countermeasure.** Same as standard. But frame it as "improving the system" not "fixing myself"
5. **Talk to someone.** RSD is worse in isolation. Text a friend. Call your therapist. Tell someone: "I'm spiraling over something small and I need to reality-check."

```
  Reminder method: ________________________

NON-NEGOTIABLE TERMS (max 3 -- keep it small):
1. ___________________________________________________

3. ___________________________________________________

ENVIRONMENT DESIGN:
- To support Term 1, my environment change is: _________
- To support Term 2, my environment change is: _________
- To support Term 3, my environment change is: _________

DISRUPTION PROTOCOL:
When my routine is broken, my fallback terms are:
1. ___________________________________________________
2. ___________________________________________________

SENSORY/ENERGY BUDGET:
- Max social hours per week: _____
- Required decompression time after social: _____ min
- Sensory accommodations I maintain: __________________

BREACH PROTOCOL (modified):
1. Name it. Write it down. Close the notebook.
2. Process it later (not now -- the emotions are lying)
3. One system fix. Not a self-fix. A system fix.
4. Read: "A breach is data, not a verdict."

SPRINT LENGTH: [ ] 1 week  [ ] 2 weeks  [ ] 4 weeks
REVIEW: _________ (day) for _____ minutes (max, not min)
```

## What Neurodivergent People Do Better

This isn't toxic positivity. These are documented cognitive differences that are genuine assets:

- **Hyperfocus** (ADHD): When directed at contract-aligned activities, produces output that neurotypical sustained attention cannot match
- **Pattern recognition** (Autism): Excellent at identifying systems, inefficiencies, and optimization opportunities -- exactly what the contract framework requires
- **Deep knowledge** (Autism special interests): Can produce expert-level skill capital in focused domains faster than generalist approaches
- **Divergent thinking** (ADHD): Generates creative solutions to problems that linear thinkers miss
- **Honesty with self** (common in both): Many neurodivergent people, having struggled with systems that weren't built for them, develop exceptional self-awareness -- exactly what the Honest Audit requires
- **Resilience** (built through experience): If you've made it this far with a brain that doesn't fit the default world, you're tougher than you think

The contract doesn't ask you to become neurotypical. It asks you to build a system that works for YOUR brain. That's different. And you're the only one who can design it.

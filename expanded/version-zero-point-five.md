# Version 0.5: The Crisis Contract

### For When You're Not Optimizing. You're Surviving.

---

## Who This Is For

This module is not for people who want to "level up." It is for people who are:

- Homeless or one paycheck from homeless
- In active addiction and want out
- In a mental health crisis -- depression so heavy that getting out of bed is the accomplishment
- Leaving an abusive situation with nothing
- Recently lost everything -- job, partner, housing, health -- in a cascade of collapse
- So deep in debt that the numbers feel impossible
- Thinking about not being alive anymore

If you're here, the standard contract (V1.0) is too much. It's not built for someone in survival mode. **Version 0.5 is.**

V0.5 strips the methodology down to its absolute minimum. No financial architecture. No sprint planning. No 5-year career arcs. Just three words:

**Stay alive. Get safe. Get stable.**

That is the entire scope of Version 0.5.

---

## Why a Contract Still Matters in Crisis

When everything has collapsed, a contract might seem absurd. "I can't even eat regularly, and you want me to sign a document?"

Yes. Here's why:

In crisis, you lose your sense of agency -- the feeling that your actions matter, that you have any control over what happens next. This is the most dangerous thing you can lose, because without agency, there's no reason to try.

A contract -- even a tiny one with a single term -- is an act of agency. It says: "I chose this. I committed to this. I did this." Even if "this" is as small as eating one real meal or making one phone call.

**The contract doesn't fix the crisis. It gives you a foothold.**

## Example V0.5 Contract Terms

These are real terms for real situations. They are not aspirational. They are the minimum viable actions for someone in crisis:

### If You're in Active Addiction:
- "I will not use today" (one day at a time -- this is the proven framework)
- "I will call one resource number this week" (SAMHSA: 1-800-662-4357)
- "I will attend one meeting this week" (AA/NA/SMART Recovery -- most are free, many are online)

### If You're in Severe Depression:
- "I will get out of bed and move to another room for at least 30 minutes today"
- "I will eat at least one real meal today" (not a bag of chips at midnight -- something with actual nutrition)
- "I will take my medication" (if prescribed)
- "I will step outside for 5 minutes today" (sunlight and fresh air are not cures, but they are small inputs that shift neurochemistry slightly in the right direction)

### If You're Homeless or Housing-Insecure:
- "I will call 211 or visit 211.org this week to identify local resources"
- "I will make contact with one shelter, program, or assistance agency this week"
- "I will keep my phone charged" (your phone is your lifeline to resources, jobs, and people)

### If You're Leaving Abuse:
- "I will call the National DV Hotline: 1-800-799-7233 (or text START to 88788)"
- "I will tell one person I trust what is happening"
- "I will secure my essential documents (ID, birth certificate, Social Security card) in a location the abuser cannot access"

### If You've Lost Your Job:
- "I will file for unemployment benefits within 48 hours"
- "I will apply for SNAP/food assistance within one week"
- "I will apply for at least one job per day" (not the perfect job -- any job that provides income while you stabilize)

### If You're in Financial Catastrophe:
- "I will open every piece of mail and every email from creditors" (avoidance is the most expensive response)
- "I will call each creditor and explain the situation" (most will work with you -- hardship programs, deferred payments, reduced rates -- but only if you call)
- "I will calculate my absolute minimum survival budget: rent, utilities, food, transportation. Everything else stops."

## Minimum Viable Existence

When you're in crisis, you need to identify and secure the absolute essentials. Everything else is deferred. Not abandoned -- deferred.

**The Survival Hierarchy:**

```
LEVEL 1: IMMEDIATE SAFETY
├── Am I physically safe right now?
├── If no: 911 / shelter / safe person's home
└── This must be resolved before anything else

LEVEL 2: BASIC NEEDS
├── Food: Am I eating at least once per day?
│   Resources: SNAP, food banks, 211.org
├── Shelter: Do I have a place to sleep tonight?
│   Resources: Local shelters, 211.org, DV hotline
├── Medical: Am I in a medical emergency?
│   Resources: ER (they must treat you regardless of ability to pay)
└── Medication: Am I taking essential prescriptions?
    Resources: GoodRx, patient assistance programs, Medicaid

LEVEL 3: STABILITY INFRASTRUCTURE
├── Phone: Is my phone working? (Essential for job search, resources)
├── ID: Do I have identification documents?
├── Income: Do I have any source of money coming in?
│   Resources: Unemployment, gig work, temp agencies, day labor
├── Hygiene: Can I shower and maintain basic personal care?
└── Transportation: Can I get to essential appointments?

LEVEL 4: READY TO UPGRADE
├── Stable housing secured
├── Consistent food access
├── Some form of income
├── Basic mental health stabilized (not perfect -- functional)
├── Immediate crisis resolved or contained
└── Ready for V1.0 contract
```

You move through these levels one at a time. You do not skip levels. You do not try to optimize Level 3 while Level 1 is unsolved.

---

## When Are You Ready for V1.0?

You're ready to upgrade from V0.5 to V1.0 when:

1. **Your immediate crisis has stabilized** (not resolved -- stabilized. You're not in freefall anymore)
2. **You can meet your V0.5 term(s) most days** (not perfectly, but more than 50%)
3. **You have the mental bandwidth to think about the next week**, not just today
4. **Basic needs are secured:** You have food, shelter, and basic safety
5. **You feel a small spark of agency** -- the sense that what you do matters, even a little

The upgrade doesn't have to be dramatic. V1.0 might be just 3 simple terms. But the shift from "survive today" to "build this week" is significant. You'll know when you're ready.

## A Note on Rock Bottom

"Rock bottom" is a myth in one specific way: there is no universal bottom. Some people's rock bottom is losing a job. Some people's is homelessness. Some people's is an ICU bed. The idea that you have to hit some dramatic low point before you can change is dangerous -- it gives you permission to keep falling.

You can decide that today -- wherever you are -- is low enough. You can write a V0.5 contract in the waiting room. In the shelter. In the car you're sleeping in. On the back of a receipt.

The contract doesn't care where you are. It only cares where you're going next.

**Today is your bottom. Not because it can't get worse. Because you decided it won't.**
